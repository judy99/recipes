Homemade Fermented Pickles

fermented, pickles, preserving
Source: https://www.farmhouseonboone.com/the-easiest-way-to-make-homemade-pickles-from-all-those-extra-garden-cucumbers/

🧂Ingredients

  • 7 mini cucumbers (or 2 medium cucumbers, sliced)
  • 4 cup s filtered water
  • 2 tbsp sea salt
  • dried bay leaves and fresh dill (optional)

👩‍🍳Instructions

  1. Wash cucumbers thoroughly. Clean a 32 oz (1 quart) wide-mouth mason jar with hot soapy water, rinse well.
  2. Pack cucumbers into the jar. Pour in enough filtered water to fully cover (approx. 4 cups).
  3. Add 2 tbsp salt. Or for precision: weigh cucumbers + water in grams, multiply by 0.035 — that's how many grams of salt you need for a 3.5% brine.
  4. Secure the lid and shake well to dissolve the salt.
  5. Add bay leaves and fresh dill if desired.
  6. Keep cucumbers fully submerged using a fermentation weight, small bowl, or a smaller jar.
  7. Leave at room temperature, away from direct sunlight, for 3–5 days. Check daily — keep cucumbers submerged, briefly open the lid each day to release pressure.
  8. Taste a pickle. If sour enough, seal tightly and move to the fridge. If you want more sourness, leave out a little longer.

📌Tips

  • Cloudy brine is completely normal — it is a natural byproduct of fermentation, not mold
  • Use filtered water; chlorine in tap water can inhibit good bacteria growth
  • Use pink Himalayan, sea, or Celtic salt — not iodized table salt (iodine kills bacteria)
  • Best cucumber varieties for crunch: Boston pickling, Kirby, Calypso, Northern pickling — small to medium size
  • Optional add-ins: garlic, peppercorns, parsley, fresh dill (about 1 head per half quart for dill pickles)
  • Save some brine from the first batch to kickstart the next one — it is already full of good bacteria
  • Can double the recipe using two quart jars or one half-gallon jar
