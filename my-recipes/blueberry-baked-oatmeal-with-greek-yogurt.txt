Title: Blueberry Baked Oatmeal with Greek Yogurt
Category: breakfast, oatmeal, baking
Rating: new

Ingredients:
2 ripe bananas
1½ cups rolled oats
2 cups blueberries, fresh or frozen
1 cup Greek yogurt
½ cup milk
¼ cup honey
1 large egg
¼ cup walnuts, chopped
1 teaspoon baking powder
1 teaspoon cinnamon
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1 pinch salt (optional)

Instructions:

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease an 8×8 inch or similar baking dish.
2. In a large bowl, mash the bananas until mostly smooth. Add Greek yogurt, milk, honey, egg, and vanilla extract. Whisk until creamy.
3. Stir in rolled oats, cinnamon, baking powder, and salt. Mix well so no dry oats remain. Gently fold in 1 cup of blueberries.
4. Pour into the baking dish. Top with the remaining 1 cup blueberries and chopped walnuts.
5. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35–40 minutes, until the center is set and the top is lightly golden. Let cool 5–10 minutes before serving.

Substitutions:

- Bananas → ¾ cup unsweetened applesauce, mashed pear, or mashed sweet potato (add 2 tbsp more honey to balance)
- Rolled oats → certified gluten-free oats, or quick oats for a softer texture
- Blueberries → raspberries, chopped strawberries, blackberries, or mixed berries
- Greek yogurt → low-fat Greek yogurt, Skyr, or plain regular yogurt
- Milk → almond, soy, oat, or any dairy milk
- Honey → maple syrup or mashed soft dates
- Egg → chia egg (1 tbsp chia seeds + 2½ tbsp water)
- Walnuts → pecans, sliced almonds, pistachios, or pumpkin seeds
- Cinnamon/vanilla → pumpkin pie spice, cardamom, almond extract, or lemon zest

Tips:

- Use very ripe bananas for natural sweetness and a softer texture
- Stir thoroughly so no dry oats stay at the bottom
- Add frozen blueberries straight from the freezer — no need to thaw
- Let it rest 5 minutes before slicing so it sets properly
- Store covered in the fridge up to 4 days
- Reheat 20–30 seconds in the microwave for a warm, soft texture
